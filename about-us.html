About UNIQLO

UNIQLO, initially established in Yamaguchi, Japan, in 1949 as a textiles manufacturer, has evolved into a renowned clothing apparel company. Today, it stands as a prominent global brand with a vast network of over 1000 stores across the world. Upholding its commitment to quality and textiles, which dates back to its inception in 1949, UNIQLO continues to revolutionize the concept of clothing.

Together, as a global fashion brand, we endeavor to inspire positive change, foster creativity, and celebrate individuality while shaping a brighter, sustainable, and fashionable tomorrow for all.

Sustainable Practices

Recycled Plastic
At UNIQLO, we embrace sustainability as a core value, and one of the ways we contribute to a greener future is by using recycled plastics in our clothing. By transforming post-consumer plastic bottles into premium fibers, we create soft, comfortable, and durable fabrics that are not only fashionable but also environmentally responsible. This innovative approach allows us to reduce our environmental footprint and promote a circular economy, where discarded materials find new life in stylish and functional apparel. With recycled plastics, we are proud to offer our customers clothing that not only looks good but also feels good, knowing that each purchase supports a more sustainable planet.

Secondhand Clothes
At UNIQLO, we believe in creating a better future through clothing recycling. Through our circular sustainability programs, we collect old garments, including down products, and transform them into new items. Moreover, we collaborate with NGOs and partners to distribute wearable used clothing to those in need, including refugees and disaster victims. Our commitment to sustainability extends to the principles of RECYCLE, REUSE, and REDUCE, driving us to make a positive impact on the world.

Eco-friendly Jeans!
BLUE CYCLE JEANS revolutionizes the industry by eliminating the necessity for vast amounts of water and manual labor typically required to achieve the faded, worn-in look. Through advanced methods, these jeans significantly decrease water consumption during the finishing process, reducing it by up to 99%*. Moreover, laser distressing replaces traditional sandpapering, relieving the workload on human hands and creating a more favorable work environment.
